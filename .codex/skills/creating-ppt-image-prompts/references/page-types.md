# Page Types and Composition Rules

## Cover

Use minimal copy, a dominant title, presenter/institution/date, and one restrained visual motif. Do not use a dashboard or dense cards.

## Agenda

Show the full section structure with clear numbering. Match the number and names of actual section dividers.

## Section Divider

Use one reusable template across the deck: large section number, Chinese section name, optional English subtitle, one-line bridge, and a light topic illustration. No body list.

## Profile and Credentials

Use a portrait placeholder or photograph region plus grouped education, experience, and research fields. Keep dates aligned and readable.

## Metrics Overview

Use a small set of large numbers with short explanations. Do not bury the metric in prose.

## Evidence or Representative Work

Use consistent evidence modules across sibling pages. Include title, venue/year/role, contribution, and takeaway. Preserve exact paper names and numeric results.

## Background and Scenario

Show a progression, ecosystem, or use-case landscape. Make the transition to the research problem explicit.

## Core Diagram

Specify reading direction, nodes, layers, arrows, feedback loops, color meaning, labels, and the takeaway. A request such as “draw a roadmap” is insufficient.

## Method or Research Content

Use problem → method → outcome, or theory → detection → mitigation → isolation. Keep sibling method pages in one layout group.

## Roadmap or Architecture

Define inputs, stages, outputs, and feedback. Keep arrows behind nodes and avoid crossed labels.

## Innovation

Use 3–4 distinct claims with concise explanations. Avoid generic slogans.

## Schedule or Gantt

Specify timeline units, task rows, phase ranges, and milestones. Accuracy matters more than decoration.

## Outcomes

Separate publications, intellectual property, systems/platforms, talent, and collaboration. Keep promised quantities exact.

## Support or Assurance

Show platform, mentors, data/scenarios, prior work, and institutional support as a coherent assurance chain.

## Closing

Echo the cover and remain minimal. Include only the requested closing statement, presenter, and date.
